Third Question:
One of the major formative questions behind the construction of computer languages is "how much do I want this language to guess at what I'm trying to do, and do it for me?"
And the truth is, it can be problematic both ways. For example, take 'Garbage Collection'

Garbage Collection is the term for a language that manages memory for you; while this can be extraordinarily helpful (you don't have to think about memory address acquisition and release) it can also be equally problematic: you may find that sometimes the language "guesses wrong" about when you want to "forget" a variable, which can be difficult to diagnose/work around.

Therefore, would you prefer that your language DOES try to be your 'personal assistant':

Or do you prefer that it DOES NOT, and you must assign/manage everything personally/manually?
